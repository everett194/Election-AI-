# Problem Statement

## Overview

Local elections often have the greatest direct effect on people’s daily lives, yet they are frequently the hardest elections to research.

Mayors, county commissioners, school board members, sheriffs, prosecutors, town council members, and other local officials make decisions about schools, housing, policing, transportation, zoning, taxes, public services, infrastructure, and economic development. However, voters—especially those living in small towns, rural communities, and less-covered local jurisdictions—often have very little accessible information about the candidates running for these offices.

This project is a local-election information and candidate-alignment platform. It helps users identify the elections relevant to their location, learn who is running, compare candidate positions, and complete a short questionnaire that estimates which candidates most closely align with their views on local issues.

The platform is intended to function as a nonpartisan voter-education tool.
